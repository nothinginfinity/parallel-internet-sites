## What is business credit?

Business credit is a credit profile tied to a business entity — not to the business owner personally. It is based on the business’s own EIN (Employer Identification Number) and tracks the business’s payment history with vendors, lenders, and trade accounts. Business credit bureaus (Dun & Bradstreet, Experian Business, Equifax Business) maintain these profiles separately from personal credit bureaus.

---

## How does business credit work for LLCs?

An LLC can establish its own business credit profile separate from the owner’s personal credit. To do this, the LLC must be properly formed, have an active EIN, a business address, a business bank account, and open accounts with vendors that report to business credit bureaus. Once those accounts are established and payments are made on time, the LLC begins building a trackable business credit history.

---

## What is the difference between business credit and personal credit?

Personal credit is tied to an individual’s Social Security Number and tracks personal borrowing and payment history. Business credit is tied to a business EIN and tracks the business’s financial relationships with vendors and lenders. Building business credit allows a business to access financing, vendor terms, and credit products based on the business’s own history — not the owner’s personal score.

## What do I need to start building business credit?

To start building business credit, you generally need:

1. A legally formed business entity (LLC or corporation)
2. An active EIN from the IRS
3. A business address (separate from personal where possible)
4. A business phone number listed in directory services
5. A business bank account
6. Vendor accounts that report to business credit bureaus

TrueBuild helps clients set up and complete each of these steps.

## Can a new LLC build business credit?

Yes. A newly formed LLC can begin building business credit once the foundational elements are in place (EIN, business address, business bank account). The process takes time — consistent on-time payments to reporting vendors and lenders are what build the profile over months. TrueBuild’s program is designed to guide new LLCs through this process step by step.

## How long does it take to build business credit?

Timelines vary depending on the business’s starting point, how quickly foundational accounts are established, and how consistently payments are made. Some clients begin to see an established profile within a few months; a strong, multi-tiered profile typically takes longer to develop. Results depend on individual business history and lender policies.

## What are the major business credit bureaus?

The three primary business credit bureaus in the United States are:

1. **Dun & Bradstreet** — maintains the PAYDEX score; requires a D-U-N-S Number
2. **Experian Business** — Intelliscore Plus
3. **Equifax Business** — Business Credit Risk Score

A complete business credit profile is ideally established with all three.
